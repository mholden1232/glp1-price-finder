The Core Difference

Ozempic and Wegovy both contain semaglutide — a GLP-1 receptor agonist that mimics a natural gut hormone to reduce appetite, slow digestion, and improve blood sugar control. They are made by the same company (Novo Nordisk) and work through the same mechanism. But they are distinct FDA-approved products with different indications and different doses.

Ozempic is approved to improve blood sugar control in adults with type 2 diabetes and to reduce cardiovascular risk. It is not approved for weight loss. Wegovy is approved specifically for chronic weight management in adults with obesity or overweight with at least one weight-related condition.

Why Do Doctors Prescribe Ozempic Off-Label for Weight Loss?

This is the source of enormous confusion. Because Ozempic and Wegovy contain the same molecule, many physicians prescribe Ozempic for weight loss even though it is only FDA-approved for diabetes. This is called off-label prescribing and is perfectly legal — doctors can prescribe any approved medication for any purpose they deem medically appropriate.

The practical reason is insurance coverage. Ozempic (a diabetes drug) is more commonly covered by insurance than Wegovy (a weight loss drug). Patients who cannot get insurance to cover Wegovy sometimes get Ozempic covered instead and use it for weight loss at their doctor's direction.

⚠️ The dose difference matters for weight loss

At the doses used for diabetes (0.5mg to 1mg weekly), Ozempic produces modest weight loss of 3 to 6% on average. Wegovy at 2.4mg weekly produces 15% average weight loss. If weight loss is your goal, the higher dose matters — which is why Wegovy was specifically developed and why compounded semaglutide at weight-loss doses (up to 2.4mg weekly) is what most telehealth weight loss programs prescribe.

Compounded Semaglutide: The Cash-Pay Alternative

Most cash-pay patients accessing semaglutide for weight loss are using compounded semaglutide — not brand-name Ozempic or Wegovy. Compounded semaglutide contains the same active ingredient, is prepared by licensed U.S. compounding pharmacies, and is prescribed at the same doses used for weight loss.

The cost difference is dramatic. Brand-name Wegovy retails at approximately $1,349 per month. Compounded semaglutide from telehealth platforms starts at $99 to $149 per month. That is an 88% cost reduction for the same molecule.

Compounded semaglutide is not FDA-approved. It is legal and widely prescribed through licensed telehealth providers, but it has not been evaluated by the FDA for safety, effectiveness, or quality. Most patients tolerate it well, but the regulatory context is worth understanding.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Ozempic the same as Wegovy?

They contain the same active ingredient — semaglutide — but are different products. Ozempic is approved for type 2 diabetes at doses up to 2mg weekly. Wegovy is approved for weight management at 2.4mg weekly. The higher Wegovy dose produces significantly more weight loss than typical Ozempic doses used for diabetes.

Can I use Ozempic for weight loss?

Doctors can legally prescribe Ozempic off-label for weight loss, and many do. However, insurance coverage for Ozempic for weight loss (rather than diabetes) varies and is often denied. For cash-pay weight loss patients, compounded semaglutide is typically the most cost-effective option.

Why is Wegovy so expensive?

Brand-name Wegovy is priced at approximately $1,349 per month at retail — the same pricing strategy Novo Nordisk uses for premium biologics. Novo Nordisk's NovoCare direct pharmacy offers introductory pricing starting at $149/month, and compounded semaglutide from telehealth platforms starts as low as $99/month.

What is the difference between Ozempic and compounded semaglutide?

Compounded semaglutide contains the same active ingredient as both Ozempic and Wegovy but is prepared by a licensed compounding pharmacy rather than manufactured by Novo Nordisk. It is not FDA-approved, which means it has not undergone FDA review for safety, effectiveness, or quality. It is significantly cheaper — typically $99 to $299/month versus $349 to $1,349/month for brand-name options.
